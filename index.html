Job Market Paper

  • “The Financial Propagation Mechanism of Commodity Booms” (with Walter Cuba)
    Draft coming soon

    • We examine the financial transmission mechanism in small open economies (SOEs) that connects commodity price booms to the non-commodity sector via banks. We propose that after a commodity price boom, exporters increase their deposits in domestic banks, prompting banks to expand loans to non-commodity firms. These firms then boost hiring. We provide empirical evidence using matched bank-firm-loan data from Peru, an SOE that experienced a commodity price boom in the 2000s. We quantify the aggregate impact of this mechanism by incorporating it into an SOE model with banks. Calibrating the model to Peruvian data, our simulations suggest the mechanism accounts for a quarter of the Peruvian GDP growth during the booming episode.
